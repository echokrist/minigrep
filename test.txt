We must discuss the Stack and the Heap once more. The Stack is a tower of truth. It is ordered. It is fast. It is predictable. When a function ends, the stack frame is wiped clean, like a chalkboard at the end of a lesson. The Heap is different. The Heap is a vast field of memory. It requires a map. It requires a pointer. If you lose the pointer, you lose the memory. This is the tragedy of the memory leak. But we do not speak of leaks here. Our code is clean. Our code is Rust.

Performance is not just a metric; it is a virtue. A slow search is a wall between the user and their data. When you optimize your filter, when you move your allocations outside the loop, you are respecting the user’s time. You are saying, "I value your clock cycles." In the Silicon Horizon, time is the only currency that matters.
